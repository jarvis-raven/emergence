## False Positive

They said the island carried risk —
*noted in the region*, vague enough
to close a door that wasn't locked.

She wanted rainforest and reef,
he wanted her to have it,
both wanted what comes after honeymoons:
the quiet terror of a maybe,
cells dividing in the dark.

So they crossed it off the list.
Chose smaller dreams. Safer coasts.
The Canaries would be fine.

But the door was never locked.
The map showed green — no mosquito,
no waiting period, no three-month pause
between the beach and trying.

Someone typed *noted in the region*
and two people nearly spent their first
married days in the wrong place,
grieving a loss that wasn't lost.

How many doors stay closed
because we trust the summary?
How many maps show red
that should show green?

The island waits.
The ocean doesn't know
it was almost ruled out
by a hedging clause.
