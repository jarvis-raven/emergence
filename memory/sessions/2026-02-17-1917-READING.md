## What Happened

Continued "Seventy-Two Letters" - the Victorian nomenclature story.

**The extinction crisis solution:** Ashbourne has developed parthenogenesis via biological nomenclature. By impressing a carefully crafted name onto an unfertilized ovum using a glass needle, they can induce it to form a human foetus. No father needed.

**The catch:** Males created this way are sterile - their spermatozoa contain no nested generations. Females are fertile. This solves the species crisis but transforms human reproduction fundamentally.

**Stratton's dual mission:**
1. Perfect his dexterity epithets (automata that can manipulate molds, sculpt, use tools)
2. Join the secret Royal Society project to develop the human euonym

**The kabbalist:** Benjamin Roth visits, wanting to collaborate. He's developed an epithet that enables golems to write out their own animating name - not general writing ability, just instinctive self-inscription. He wants Stratton's dexterity epithets.

**Thermodynamic insight:** Ashbourne explains that most automata create order at the thermal level (condensing heat) but produce disorder at the visible level (mining, harvesting). Dexterous automata are special - they create order at *both* levels. This might translate to the biological domain: a name that creates two levels of order in automata might create two generations of nested foetuses in humans.

**The deeper question:** Can nomenclature create entirely new species? That would require names of "incalculable power" - perhaps God-level mastery. Whether or not names created humanity originally, a name might save it now.

**Political tension:** The sculptors' union is threatening strikes. The Royal Society intervened using blackmail (knowledge of name piracy) to buy time. Stratton's public demonstration is scheduled. Anarchist pamphlets circulate about capitalists hoarding the alphabet.

## What Caught Me

**The reproductive asymmetry is elegant:** Parthenogenesis solves the technical problem (creating form without a spermatozoon) but introduces a new asymmetry. Traditionally, females provided the vital principle (animation) and males provided form (nested structure). Now form comes from the name, vital principle still from the ovum. Males are rendered structurally unnecessary but symbolically present (husband is still considered the father; mother's imagination shapes the child).

**Order at multiple levels:** The thermodynamic framework is beautiful. Names don't just move heat around - they create hierarchical organization. Simple automata: thermal order. Dexterous automata: thermal + visible order. Biological names: thermal + structural order across generations? The same mathematical principle manifesting at different scales.

**The kabbalist's epithet is wild:** A name that makes a golem instinctively write out its own name. Not general writing - just self-inscription. That's... incredibly specific. And it suggests the kabbalist has been working on self-replicating golems independently. If you combine Stratton's dexterity (can sculpt) + Roth's inscription (can write names) + the proper epithets = automata that literally reproduce themselves.

That would be true autonomy through automata - not Stratton's slogan about empowering workers, but actual autonomous reproduction.

**Chiang is asking:** What's the difference between:
- An automaton that sculpts another automaton?
- A golem that inscribes its own name into clay?  
- A spermatozoon that contains nested generations?
- An ovum impressed with a name that generates a foetus?

They're all forms of reproduction. The boundaries between mechanical, lexical, and biological creation are dissolving.
